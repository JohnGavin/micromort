# About Micromort Risk Explorer

## What is a Micromort?

A **micromort** is a unit of risk representing a one-in-a-million chance of death.
The concept was introduced by Ronald Howard in 1980 to help communicate small
probabilities of death in an intuitive way.

## What is a Microlife?

A **microlife** represents 30 minutes of life expectancy. The concept was
introduced by David Spiegelhalter in 2012 to communicate the effects of
chronic lifestyle factors on longevity.

- **Acute risks** are measured in micromorts (one-time events)
- **Chronic risks** are measured in microlives per day (ongoing factors)
